## Purpose

A cover letter is a place to sell relevant skills and show interest in a position. It can highlight achievements that make a candidate a strong fit for the job.

## When to include one

- If a job posting asks for a cover letter, send one.
- If it is optional, a cover letter may help show the match to the job, explain employment gaps or issues, or explain a special interest in the role or employer.
- If an employer asks for a résumé by email, the email body can be used as a cover letter.

## How to write a good cover letter

Tailor the letter for each position. A useful letter can be about 200–400 words in three or four short paragraphs.

### Recommended sections

1. **Heading and greeting:** Include the date, name, phone number, email address, and city where relevant. Address a named person where possible. If no name is available, use a relevant job title or a suitable recipient such as Human Resources or Search Committee.
2. **Brief introduction:** State who you are, why you are writing, and how you found the position. Express genuine energy and interest.
3. **Highlights:** In two or three paragraphs, summarize skills, education, experience, and accomplishments that could help the employer. Explain why you want to work for that employer.
4. **Closing:** Thank the reader, state an appropriate follow-up or that you look forward to hearing from them, and close professionally (for example, “Sincerely”).

## Tailoring and AI assistance

One tailoring technique is a “T” format: place an employer requirement beside the matching qualification. When using an AI tool to create a draft, revise it so the final letter accurately reflects the candidate’s own voice, skills, experience, and interest in the role.

## Template outline

- Your name, phone, email, date
- Hiring manager name, title, organization, address
- Greeting
- Paragraph 1: role, source of opening, and interest in the employer
- Paragraphs 2–3: relevant strengths and evidence; relate these to employer needs
- Final paragraph: mention the attached résumé, show interest in discussing the role, and thank the reader
- Professional closing and name
